That 2pm wall isn't about sleep. After a high-spike meal, your glucose drops below where it started — your body reads that as an emergency. Cue fatigue, fog, and the snack drawer.

Constant cravings

Every spike comes with a drop. Your brain reads the drop as "we need energy now" — even if you ate an hour ago. Steadier curves, fewer cravings.

Glucose drops at night trigger cortisol and adrenaline. Wired but tired? Waking at 3am? The kitchen is upstream of the bedroom.

Your brain runs on glucose. When levels swing, so do your neurotransmitters. Steadier glucose, steadier mood — without changing who you are.
